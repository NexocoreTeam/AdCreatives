# Strategy Matrix: OLIPOP

*1 persona(s) × 5 Schwartz awareness stages = 5 messaging cells*

## The Guilty Soda Lover *(primary)*

### Unaware

**Angle:** The soda you loved as a kid didn't have to be the villain — the formula was just wrong.

**What they know:** They drink soda occasionally and feel vaguely bad about it, or they swapped to sparkling water and consider the matter settled. They haven't connected gut health to their daily drink choices at all — prebiotics are a supplement-aisle concept, not a soda-aisle one.

**Gap to fill:** That the soda-versus-health tradeoff is a false binary. A soda exists that actually feeds the microbiome with 9g of plant fiber and real botanical ingredients — and it tastes like the root beer or cola they grew up on.

**Hook style:** pattern-interrupt / nostalgic-reveal

**Example hook:** *"Nobody told you soda could have 9 grams of fiber. Honestly, nobody told us either — so we made it."*

**Framework:** `bab` | **Mechanic:** Nostalgic B-roll Reframe — open on warm, slow-motion footage of a classic soda being poured (the sound, the fizz, the visual ritual), then cut to a single OLIPOP can in the same shot. Voice-over: 'What if soda was always supposed to taste this good AND do something good?' No clinical language. Let the visual do the before/after work silently.

**Proof to surface:** OLIPOP's OLIPOP Blend: cassava root, chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, nopal cactus, marshmallow root — 9g plant fiber per can. Leads with ingredients from nature, not a lab.

**CTA:** Discover OLIPOP | **Funnel:** cold

*Notes: Do NOT open with gut health or prebiotics — this audience hasn't made that mental connection yet. Lead with soda nostalgia and let the functional reveal land second. Tone must stay warm and playful, never preachy. OLIPOP's aesthetic edge is the retro-groovy can design — use it prominently as a visual pattern interrupt in a feed full of clinical wellness content.*

### Problem Aware

**Angle:** You gave up soda trying to be healthier. You shouldn't have had to.

**What they know:** They know they're drinking too much regular soda, or they're unsatisfied with the sparkling water swap they've made. They feel the tension: soda tastes good, health matters, nothing in between has felt like a real solution. Diet soda feels sketchy. Plain sparkling water feels like a consolation prize.

**Gap to fill:** That OLIPOP exists as a third category — not a health supplement, not a compromise, but a real soda experience with 2–5g of sugar and functional gut health ingredients baked into the formula.

**Hook style:** empathy-callout / story-opener

**Example hook:** *"You switched to sparkling water. You told yourself it was fine. It wasn't fine, was it."*

**Framework:** `pas` | **Mechanic:** Talking Head Confession — a relatable person (UGC-style, not overly polished) describes the exact ritual they miss: cracking a cold Coke at a barbecue, root beer with a burger, cream soda on a Friday night. They're not dramatic — just honest. Then they hold up an OLIPOP Classic Root Beer or Vintage Cola and say 'This is the first time in two years I actually look forward to what I'm drinking.' No list of benefits. One felt truth.

**Proof to surface:** OLIPOP has 2–5g of sugar versus 39g in a standard Coca-Cola. The swap is real, not symbolic. Surface this as a felt benefit ('same ritual, a fraction of the sugar') rather than a stat dump.

**CTA:** Try It for Yourself | **Funnel:** cold

*Notes: This is likely OLIPOP's highest-volume cold audience — people actively searching for soda alternatives or consuming wellness content about cutting sugar. The emotional register here is validation, not education. Don't explain prebiotics yet. The hook is 'we see you and we solved it.' Classic Root Beer and Vintage Cola are the right hero products at this stage — they carry the maximum nostalgia payload.*

### Solution Aware

**Angle:** Not all prebiotic sodas are built the same — OLIPOP is the one that actually tastes like soda and actually feeds your gut.

**What they know:** They know 'healthier soda' is a category. They may have seen Poppi on TikTok, noticed OLIPOP at Whole Foods, or heard a friend talk about prebiotic drinks. They're exploring options but haven't committed — and they carry skepticism about whether any of these actually taste like real soda.

**Gap to fill:** Why OLIPOP's formula is meaningfully different from Poppi and other competitors, and specifically why the OLIPOP Blend (plant fiber + prebiotics + botanicals) is more robust than ACV-based alternatives. Also: that OLIPOP's flavor library was built to replicate the exact nostalgia of classic American soda, not just gesture at it.

**Hook style:** comparison-contrast / category-education

**Example hook:** *"There are prebiotic sodas that taste like compromise. And then there's the one that made it onto the Today Show and sold out at Target."*

**Framework:** `fab` | **Mechanic:** Side-by-Side Without Comment — show two cans (OLIPOP vs. a generic 'healthy soda' silhouette) with ingredient panels visible. OLIPOP's reads: cassava root, chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, nopal cactus, marshmallow root. The other reads: apple cider vinegar, stevia, natural flavors. No voiceover needed. Then cut to someone taking a sip of OLIPOP Classic Root Beer and their face doing all the talking. End card: '9g plant fiber. 2–5g sugar. Tastes like the real thing.'

**Proof to surface:** Today Show feature; wide retail presence at Whole Foods, Target, Kroger (signals mainstream legitimacy, not fringe wellness); the OLIPOP Blend specificity (named plant-based fiber sources) vs. vague 'prebiotic' language from competitors.

**CTA:** See What's Inside | **Funnel:** cold_warm

*Notes: This is the stage where OLIPOP can pull away from Poppi without ever naming them. The differentiator to emphasize is formula depth (plant fiber blend vs. ACV) and flavor authenticity (classic soda nostalgia vs. trend-chasing). Avoid making this feel like a head-to-head attack ad — OLIPOP's tone is too warm for that. Let the ingredient story and the taste reaction do the competitive work implicitly.*

### Product Aware

**Angle:** Every objection you have about OLIPOP is exactly what we expected — here's what happened when skeptics tried it anyway.

**What they know:** They know OLIPOP exists. They've probably seen it in a store, scrolled past an ad, or heard about it. They haven't bought yet — most likely because of one of four objections: it won't taste like real soda, it's too expensive, they've been burned by other 'healthy' versions before, or they don't understand what prebiotics actually do for them.

**Gap to fill:** That the taste objection collapses on first sip. That at roughly $2.50 per can they're getting functional gut support that would cost multiples more in supplement form. That over 1,000 five-star reviews on the site validate the taste claim from people who started with the exact same skepticism.

**Hook style:** objection-first / social-proof-cascade

**Example hook:** *"'I was 100% sure it would taste like a health drink. I was wrong.' — every OLIPOP skeptic, after their first Classic Root Beer."*

**Framework:** `pastor` | **Mechanic:** Skeptic-to-Convert UGC Stack — compile 3–4 short first-reaction clips from real customers, each opening with their specific objection ('I thought it would taste fake,' 'I figured it was just sparkling water with marketing,' 'I couldn't justify the price') and ending with their genuine reaction after trying it. No scripting, no polish. Close with a single product shot: OLIPOP Classic Root Beer or Vintage Cola, with the 'Microbiome Approved' badge and the star rating visible. The format does the persuasion work; OLIPOP just curates the proof.

**Proof to surface:** 5-star review volume on drinkolipop.com; Today Show feature (mainstream credibility anchor); retail availability at Target and Whole Foods (accessibility and trust signal — 'if Target carries it, it's real'); specific review language about taste ('tastes exactly like the real thing' style verbatims).

**CTA:** Read the Reviews / Try Your First Case | **Funnel:** warm_retargeting

*Notes: Price objection should be addressed indirectly, not defensively — frame value through the functional ingredient angle ('9g plant fiber per can; a prebiotic supplement alone would cost you more per serving') rather than discounting or apologizing. If OLIPOP is running a variety pack promotion, this is the right stage to surface it — trial risk feels lower with a variety pack than committing to one flavor. Classic Root Beer is the safest first-flavor recommendation for this persona because it carries the strongest nostalgia hook.*

### Most Aware

**Angle:** You already know OLIPOP is your soda. Stock the fridge.

**What they know:** They know OLIPOP, they want it, they've probably had it before or added it to their cart. They need a final reason to act now — a nudge, an offer, a seasonal hook, a flavor they haven't tried, or simply a reminder that the thing they already like is right there waiting.

**Gap to fill:** About new flavors (Raspberry Sherbet, Blackberry Vanilla), ongoing offers (Shirley Temple at 30% off), or variety pack options that make stocking up feel smart rather than indulgent.

**Hook style:** direct-offer / new-flavor-reveal

**Example hook:** *"Raspberry Sherbet just dropped and it tastes exactly like summer in a can. Your fridge is about to get a lot more fun."*

**Framework:** `slap` | **Mechanic:** Flavor Reveal with Lifestyle Payoff — fast, punchy, visually led. Open on a single OLIPOP can (Raspberry Sherbet or Blackberry Vanilla) being pulled from a fully stocked, aesthetically organized fridge. No long setup. Voiceover or text overlay: the flavor name, one evocative descriptor ('like a sherbet push-pop, but make it grown-up'), and the offer or variety pack CTA. Ends on brand: groovy retro illustration style, warm palette, the sound of a can cracking open. This is a 10-second ad, not a 30-second one.

**Proof to surface:** New flavor launches (Raspberry Sherbet, Blackberry Vanilla called out as fan favorites in site announcement bar); Shirley Temple 30% off promotional pricing; Variety Pack framing (Classic Soda, Fruity Fun, Favorites) for stocking-up behavior; implied social proof via 'fan favorite' language.

**CTA:** Shop Now / Stock Your Fridge | **Funnel:** retargeting

*Notes: This audience does not need education or persuasion — they need permission and occasion. Make it easy and make it fun. Lean into OLIPOP's retro-groovy aesthetic at full volume here — this is the stage where the brand's visual identity can do the heaviest lifting with no explanatory copy needed. Seasonal and limited-edition angles (SpongeBob Pineapple Paradise, Shirley Temple) work especially well at this stage as urgency drivers without resorting to aggressive discount language.*

## Cross-stage observations

**Highest leverage stages:** problem_aware — This is where the largest cold audience lives. Health-conscious adults actively trying to reduce sugar or find a soda alternative represent a massive, targetable, scroll-ready audience. OLIPOP's emotional hook (you gave up soda trying to be healthy; you shouldn't have had to) is both true and deeply resonant here. Volume of creative investment should be highest at this stage., product_aware — The taste objection is OLIPOP's biggest conversion blocker, and it collapses on first sip. The gap between 'aware but unconverted' and 'bought' is almost entirely a trust gap that UGC and social proof close efficiently. High ROI on creative investment at this stage because the persuasion work is done by customers, not the brand.

**Weakest stages:** unaware — OLIPOP's product is inherently easier to sell to people who already feel the tension between wanting soda and wanting to be healthy. Reaching truly unaware audiences (people who haven't connected gut health to their daily drink ritual at all) requires higher spend and more patience. The category education burden is real., solution_aware — This stage requires OLIPOP to differentiate from Poppi and other prebiotic sodas without triggering brand defensiveness or feeling clinical. The OLIPOP Blend is a genuine differentiator but requires nuanced communication — ingredient lists don't convert; felt benefits do. Creative execution here is harder to get right.

**Budget/creative distribution:** Allocate roughly 40% of creative volume and budget to problem_aware (largest cold audience, strongest emotional hook, clearest path to trial), 25% to product_aware (high ROI on UGC-driven objection handling), 20% to most_aware (retention, repeat purchase, new flavor adoption), 10% to solution_aware (competitive differentiation, category education), and 5% to unaware (brand equity, funnel seeding). If budget is constrained, deprioritize unaware first — OLIPOP's retail presence (Target, Whole Foods) does meaningful unaware work passively.

**Category notes:** OLIPOP competes in a noisy, fast-moving functional beverage category where taste credibility is the primary purchase barrier and aesthetic differentiation is table stakes. Three category-specific dynamics to build into creative strategy: (1) Taste proof must be visceral and human, not statistical — a single genuine reaction shot outperforms any ingredient callout for conversion at cold stages. (2) Nostalgia is OLIPOP's genuine structural advantage over Poppi and every other competitor in the space — Classic Root Beer and Vintage Cola are not just flavors, they are the brand's proof of concept that healthy soda doesn't mean compromised soda. These two SKUs should anchor acquisition creative even as newer flavors (Raspberry Sherbet, Blackberry Vanilla) anchor retention and newness creative. (3) The gut health benefit is best communicated as a silent ingredient story rather than a medical claim — 'plant fiber from cassava root and chicory root' lands warmer and more credibly than 'supports your microbiome.' OLIPOP's 'Microbiome Approved' badge on-can does this elegantly; creative should borrow the same tone.
