## Brand Overview

OLIPOP is a better-for-you prebiotic soda that delivers the nostalgic taste of classic soda with a functional twist: plant fiber, prebiotics, and botanical ingredients that support digestive health. Founded by Ben Goodwin and David Lester, OLIPOP sits at the intersection of the $400B soda category and the booming gut-health wellness trend — positioned not as a health supplement, but as a genuinely delicious soda that happens to be good for you. It sells DTC via its Shopify store and has achieved wide retail distribution through Whole Foods, Target, Kroger, and beyond.

---

## Brand Story & Origin

OLIPOP was co-founded by Ben Goodwin and David Lester, who shared a vision of creating a healthier, tastier alternative to conventional soda. Ben Goodwin — whose name appears in one of the brand's playful flavor SKUs ("Doctor Goodwin") — has been the public face of the brand and its gut-health science story. The origin is rooted in the belief that soda doesn't have to be junk: that it could taste exactly like the sodas people grew up loving while actually doing something good for the body.

Their stated mission (from the Our Story page): *"a healthier, tastier future"* — a vision that led to building a formula combining plant fiber, prebiotics, and botanicals that work together to support the microbiome. The brand launched as a DTC-first company and has scaled aggressively into retail.

OLIPOP has been growing rapidly since roughly 2019–2020 and is now one of the most visible brands in the functional beverage and better-for-you soda space.

## What Makes Them Different

OLIPOP's core differentiation is its **functional formula** combined with its **real soda taste and experience** — a combination its competitors in the healthy soda space haven't fully nailed.

From their own site language:
- *"Healthy Prebiotic Soda"* — the brand frames itself not as a soda alternative, but as a **new kind of soda**
- *"Made with plant fiber and prebiotics"*
- *"Non-GMO, gluten-free, paleo, and vegan"*
- *"High fiber and 2–5g of sugar"*
- *"Delicious, refreshing and classic flavors"*

The proprietary differentiator is their **OLIPOP Blend** — a combination of plant-based dietary fiber (cassava root, chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, nopal cactus, marshmallow root) plus prebiotics. This is their unique mechanism: it feeds the gut microbiome using ingredients from nature, not lab-synthesized additives.

They position this as *"Microbiome Approved"* — a phrase that appears as a visual badge on their cans. The brand wants the formula to feel credible without making medical claims.

Key claim structure: functional ingredients + real soda taste + better-for-you credentials (Non-GMO, gluten-free, paleo, vegan, low sugar) — all in one can.

## The Alternative Solution

Before OLIPOP, the customer was doing one of the following:

1. **Drinking regular Coke/Pepsi/Dr Pepper** — and feeling guilty about it, or actively trying to cut back
2. **Drinking sparkling water** (LaCroix, Spindrift, Bubly) — but missing the sweetness and soda flavor of real soda
3. **Drinking diet soda** — and worrying about artificial sweeteners
4. **Giving up soda entirely** — and feeling deprived at meals, parties, or when craving something nostalgic

The "old way" this product replaces is fundamentally **guilt-associated soda consumption** or **unsatisfying soda substitutes**. OLIPOP's role is to collapse the trade-off: you can have the soda you want AND it can be good for your gut.

## Core Audience(s)

**Primary:** Health-conscious Millennials and Gen Z adults (approximately 25–40 years old) who grew up drinking soda, are now cleaning up their diets, and are either in the gut-health-curious space or actively trying to reduce sugar. Often: urban or suburban, disposable income, shops at Whole Foods or Target, reads ingredient labels. Skews female but has meaningful male crossover.

**Secondary A — Health-forward Moms:** Parents (primarily moms) looking for a better option they can feel good about serving their families. The family-safe brand voice and recognizable flavor names (Root Beer, Cream Soda, Grape) make it easy to sell to kids by proxy.

**Secondary B — Nostalgic Soda Lovers in Wellness Transition:** Adults who are deeply connected to the ritual and taste of soda — not looking for a "health drink," but open to a soda that doesn't make them feel bad. The "new kind of soda" framing speaks directly to this audience.

**Secondary C — Gut Health Enthusiasts:** People actively managing gut health, IBS, or digestive issues who are interested in prebiotic supplementation in a more enjoyable format than capsules or kombucha.

**Key audience signals from site language and product framing:**
- "Discover a new kind of soda" — speaks to the soda-lover who's already skeptical of health drinks
- "Healthy, happy you" — wellness-forward but not clinical
- Flavor names like Root Beer, Cream Soda, Grape — classic Americana nostalgia cues
- SpongeBob collab — cultural relevance to Millennials (grew up with SpongeBob) and parents buying for kids
- "Shirley Temple" flavor — nostalgic, family-friendly

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Overall:** Playful, warm, confident, and science-adjacent — but never clinical or preachy. OLIPOP talks to you like a friend who happens to know a lot about gut health.

**Tone adjectives:** Optimistic. Nostalgic. Approachable. A little whimsical. Credible without being boring.

**Voice patterns observed from site copy:**
- Direct, punchy copy ("Discover a new kind of soda")
- Benefit-forward without being lecture-y ("made with plant fiber and prebiotics")
- Claim-stacking done cleanly ("Non-GMO, gluten-free, paleo, and vegan, with high fiber and 2–5g of sugar")
- Warmth in product framing ("for a happy, healthy you")
- Playful flavor naming ("Doctor Goodwin," "Ridge Rush," "Pineapple Paradise")
- Community-anchoring through flavor collaborations (SpongeBob, Shirley Temple)

**What they avoid:**
- Fear-based or shame-based messaging around sugar/health
- Clinical/pharmaceutical language
- Aggressive competitor callouts
- Anything that sounds like a supplement brand

**Visual voice extension:** The brand's visual identity amplifies the written voice — bubbly, arched logotype; retro-modern cartoon illustrations; "Microbiome Approved" badge that looks like a fun seal of approval, not a medical certification. The design language is "Schoolhouse Rock meets a DTC beverage brand" — approachable, fun, and credible all at once.

## Must-Know Strategic Context

**1. The core creative tension:**
OLIPOP's #1 job in advertising is to collapse the belief that healthy and delicious are mutually exclusive. The customer's internal objection is: *"It can't actually taste like real soda if it's good for me."* Every creative concept should be built to destroy that objection.

**2. The visual identity is a conversion asset:**
OLIPOP's can design is distinctive, beautiful, and immediately recognizable. The product IS the creative — showing the can in context (cold, condensation, vivid flavor-coded colors) is one of the most effective things an ad can do. The retro-groovy aesthetic is also a scroll-stopper on social.

**3. Flavor breadth is both a strength and a challenge:**
With 20+ flavors, OLIPOP has something for everyone — but this also means ads often need to anchor around a specific flavor or flavor type rather than "all our sodas." The best creative tends to be flavor-specific (Root Beer for nostalgia, Tropical Punch for summer, Shirley Temple for family occasions).

**4. The SpongeBob collaboration signals cultural strategy:**
Collabs like SpongeBob's Pineapple Paradise and the Shirley Temple flavor show OLIPOP leans into cultural IP and nostalgia to drive virality and expand reach. Creative that taps into this nostalgia energy tends to perform.

**5. Subscription is a priority business model:**
The site prominently features a "Subscribe" nav link, subscription discount codes (30RESUB, THIRTYSUB), and 15–30% subscription discounts. Subscription conversion is clearly a core CRO goal. Any ads targeting repeat purchasers or retention should push the subscription angle.

**6. Retail presence is broad and growing:**
Whole Foods, Target, Kroger. This matters for creative — "find it at your nearest Target" style local inventory ads are on-brand and likely used. Also means OLIPOP can leverage "you've probably seen it in stores" social proof.

**7. The gut health space is crowded but underexplained:**
Most consumers know "gut health is important" but don't know what prebiotics do or how they differ from probiotics. OLIPOP has an opportunity to own simple, engaging education — but must do so without crossing into medical claim territory.

**8. Ben Goodwin as a founder asset:**
The founder's name is literally embedded in a product (Doctor Goodwin). He is a recognizable face in the brand's history and could be a UGC/founder-story creative angle, though this needs to be confirmed as an active strategy.

**9. Seasonal and limited drops drive urgency:**
The promotional banner structure (20+ active discount codes) and seasonal flavor strategy suggest OLIPOP runs frequent promotional campaigns and uses limited-time urgency effectively.

**10. Competitor battle is primarily vs. Poppi:**
Both are prebiotic sodas with strong DTC + retail presence. The creative battlefield is primarily about which brand "owns" the gut-health soda category in the consumer's mind. OLIPOP's science story (plant fiber + prebiotic blend vs. ACV) is more robust — but Poppi has been louder on paid social. This is a key strategic battleground.
